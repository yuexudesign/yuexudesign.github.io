Background information

For small independent business owners that are bothered by the unfair advantage of large corporate competitors, Fypus empowers these owners to aggregate purchases to reach economies of scale and level the playing field via an application.

Unlike anonymous, traditional group purchasing organizations that only focus on one particular industry or indirect cost, Fypus provides the platform to ally small business owners with their network of business-owning colleagues they already know and trust to source from their network of suppliers that are already known and trusted.

Fypus was seeking help from the Cornell team to create an application of a purchasing platform that allows businesses to group their supplies together and makes joining together as easy as Facebook group. Partnered businesses have the opportunity to group as many supplies as necessary and can source from whatever supplier decided upon. Fypus would aggregate their purchases and allow each self-selected group to send the total amount to any agreed upon supplier.

Usability testing

Because of the time constraint of the project, we only had time to conduct one round of usability testing on 3 participants and had some key findings:

  • On the "join" page, there is no information about why one should join Fypus and its benefit.
  • When type in items to purchase, users wanted to see a drop down menu and type ahead with items suggestions for them to find items more easily.
  • There should be a confirmation page with all the info user put in once he/she finish recruiting member.
  • Need instruction on what “create new league” means.
  • One user suggested that on the invitation email sent by Fypus, besides an introduction about Fypus, it might be helpful to include a survey on what items the user is interested in purchasing, and then connect the user with people who has purchased the same item. (Backlog)

"It's a very interesting application, like Facebook or other kinds of social media for small businesses."

We then drafted an usability testing plan for the client to conduct on the design after my team rolls off the project.

The usability test objectives are:

  • To identify design inconsistencies and usability issues within the user interface, the content and the information architecture.
  • Exercise the application under controlled test conditions with users. Collected data will be used to evaluate the usability goals of having an effective, efficient, and satisfying purchasing platform for small business owners.
  • Establish baseline user performance and user-satisfaction levels of the user interface for future usability evaluations.

Potential users should include small business owners who are making purchases of supplies by using modern technologies, such as website or app. At least 5 subjects should be participating in this study.

Usability Tasks:

  • Task 1: Request to join Fypus
  • Task 2: Create a new league
  • Task 3: Recruit members
  • Task 4: Check the latest notifications
  • Task 5: Check your leagues

Questionnaires:

  1. General feedback on the app
  2. What are some pain points when completing the task?
  3. Where did the user get stuck?

MVP flow 1: Pre-league (cold/organic search) landing page through invitation

As an interested small business owner or employee, I want to learn about Fypus so that I can request an invitation for me and other small businesses I know to join.

MVP flow 2: Fypus invitation to lead through referrals

As a small and medium business (SMB) invitee, I want to easily and quickly create a league, enter supplies to scale and immediately invite and convince other SMBs to join so that we can reach economies of scale and gain negotiating power.

MVP flow 3: Invitation from member through league scaling

As a SMB league member invitee, I want to easily join the league, enter scaling amount and invite others so that we can reach economies of scale and increase our negotiating power.

MVP flow 4: New league formed by scaling new supply or by inviting new members

As an existing SMB league member, I want to start a new league and scale a new product so that I can reach economies of scale and increase my negotiating power.

Backlog: Fypus account dashboard and chat

As an exsiting SMB league member, I log into my account dashboard and view the league profile. I also want to check my messages.
